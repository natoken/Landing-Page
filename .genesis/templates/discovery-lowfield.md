# Lowfield Discovery Guide

<!-- GUIDANCE: Lowfield projects never "ship" — they grow continuously. Discovery focuses on understanding the domain, content types, and recurring workflows. -->

## 1. Domain & Purpose

- What is this project about? (Novel, research, knowledge base, design system, etc.)
- What is the ongoing goal? (Tell a story, accumulate knowledge, document a system, etc.)
- Who is the audience? (Yourself? Readers? A team? The public?)
- What does "success" look like for this project? (Completed novel? Comprehensive wiki? Living reference?)

## 2. Content Types

- What kinds of documents or artifacts will you create?
  - Examples for a novel: chapters, character sheets, world lore, timeline, outline
  - Examples for research: entries, literature reviews, methodology notes, data catalogs, findings
  - Examples for a knowledge base: notes, topic pages, indexes, connection maps
- For each content type:
  - What information does it contain?
  - How long is a typical entry?
  - How does it relate to other content types?

## 3. Organizational Structure

- How should content be organized?
  - By topic/category?
  - By chronology?
  - By type?
  - Hierarchical or flat?
- What naming conventions should be used for files?
- Are there tags, categories, or metadata to track?
- How should cross-references between entries work?

## 4. Recurring Workflows

- What activities happen regularly?
  - Daily writing sessions?
  - Weekly reviews?
  - Ongoing research additions?
  - Periodic reorganization?
- What is the typical flow for creating a new piece of content?
- Are there review or editing cycles?
- What triggers new content creation?

## 5. Reference Materials

- What existing materials inform this project?
  - Existing drafts or notes?
  - Research papers or books?
  - Worldbuilding notes?
  - Style guides or templates?
- Should any existing material be imported or integrated?

## 6. Tools & Formats

- What is the primary format? (Markdown, plain text, LaTeX, etc.)
- What editor or platform will be used? (VS Code, Obsidian, Scrivener, etc.)
- Are there export needs? (PDF, EPUB, HTML, Word?)
- Any special formatting requirements? (Academic citations, footnotes, diagrams?)

## 7. Quality Standards

- What makes content "good" in this domain?
  - For fiction: voice consistency, plot coherence, character authenticity
  - For research: accuracy, proper citations, methodological rigor
  - For knowledge bases: completeness, freshness, discoverability
- What review process ensures quality?
- What are the common mistakes to avoid?
- Are there style or voice guidelines?
